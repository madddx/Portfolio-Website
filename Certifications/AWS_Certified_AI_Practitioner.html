Overview

About This Certification

The AWS Certified AI Practitioner certification validates a foundational understanding of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and generative AI concepts and their practical applications on the AWS cloud platform.

This credential demonstrates the ability to identify appropriate AI/ML technologies for real-world business problems, understand the lifecycle of ML models, and work with AWS AI/ML services for building, deploying, and managing intelligent applications at scale.

It reflects a strong foundation in responsible AI principles, including bias awareness, explainability, and governance frameworks for enterprise AI deployments.

By earning this certification, I have demonstrated competency in understanding how generative AI models are constructed and fine-tuned, how foundation models power modern AI applications, and how cloud infrastructure enables scalable AI solutions.

This certification complements my hands-on experience with AI/ML-driven projects and research, reinforcing my capability to architect and contribute to intelligent systems using AWS services such as Amazon SageMaker, Amazon Bedrock, Rekognition, Comprehend, and Lex.

Exam Content

Knowledge Domains

Domain 1 · 20%

Fundamentals of AI and ML

Core concepts of AI, machine learning, deep learning, and the types of ML problems including supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

Domain 2 · 24%

Fundamentals of Generative AI

Architecture of foundation models, large language models, prompt engineering, fine-tuning techniques, and real-world generative AI applications.

Domain 3 · 28%

Applications of Foundation Models

Selecting and deploying foundation models using Amazon Bedrock, model evaluation strategies, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and agents.

Domain 4 · 14%

Guidelines for Responsible AI

Responsible AI practices, bias detection, transparency, explainability, data privacy, and governance frameworks for AI systems.

Domain 5 · 14%

Security, Compliance & Governance

Security best practices for AI workloads on AWS, compliance considerations, identity management, and monitoring AI applications responsibly.
